# Combat Loop
## Posturing
This is all the actions that take place before the Generals commit to the attack.
Posturing includes all of the context around the battle, this includes terrain modifiers, how the campaign has played and history between the two forces.

### Perks
Harried: This army will initiate (the army can not run) and may have levels of exhaustion
Provoked: This army will initiate (they have an objective they must acheive)
High ground: This Rank gains increased endurance, attack and defense (scalar)
Ambushed: The army is in disarray and reserves will not be present at the start of the engagement

## Engagement
Engagement is when two combatants meet. Each rank will only have one engagement at a time.
When two combatants engage the goal is to pierce the enemy. This involves breaking their main line in two. This will result in heavy losses and potentially even route the remaining soldiers.
When combatants fight they will apply their attack against the enemies defence reducing the endurance of each.
When attacks are successful they start building holes in the enemy. When a hole is is large enough it will evetually pierce the enemy.

### Phases
Phases control what actions the troop has and how those actions occur. It influences the perks that trigger and the proc times of the actions.
#### Unit Actions
* Push
* Block
* Strike
* Change Phase

## Reinforcement
When a force begins to get holes or is pierced while there are reinforcements they will move in to help fill the gaps.
If a rank can not be reinforced that rank is infiltrated which can route or disarray other ranks and allow the infiltrated rank to swarm the enemy.
Rank infiltration will affect adjacent ranks more.
If the reinforcements have to go run through a pierced enemy they may become in disarray.
Sometimes reinforcements will not be used as they are a retainer for the King and ensuring his escape is more important.

# Units
Units have strike, power, block, formation and endurance.
## Unit Types
## Infantry
### Perks
* Shield wall: Increased endurance while engaged
* Extra rank: Increased the amount of holes required before being pierced
* Tip of the spear: Power will be focused on gaps already created
## Cavalry
### Perks
* Outflanked: The opposing units are unable to swarm the enemy from flank ranks
* Overwhelming force: The opposing unit is put into disarray (before the charge occurs)
## Projectiles
### Perks
* Pinned: The opposing unit has decided to halt
* Punctured: The opposing unit is in disarray
* Barrage: The opposing unit will initiate and may be in disarry

# Glossary
Strike: Ability to inflict damage
Power: Pressure applied to the shape of your opponents unit
Block: Ability to block strikes
Formation: The ability to regain shape
Endurance: Ability to continue to apply Power and Strikes

Swarm: When more one unit has to fight multiple units at a time
Disarray: The unit is jumbled up which can include some missing from the front and some in the 3rd row
